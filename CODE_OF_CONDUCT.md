# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — maintainers and contributors — pledge to make participation in this
project a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless
of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing when we make mistakes
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Project-specific notes

This project is opinionated about engineering discipline. **Direct,
substantive technical critique is welcome and expected.** "Your code
violates Pre-Delegation Non-Negotiable" is fine. "You're an idiot for
proposing this" is not.

Disagreement with design decisions (Single-Class skills, Buddy=Dispatcher
rule, hook-driven enforcement) is fair game for technical discussion.
Persistent demands to overturn already-decided architectural choices
without new evidence are not — see [`docs/reviews/council/`](docs/reviews/council/)
for the decision history.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards
of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective
action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening,
offensive, or harmful.

## Enforcement

Reports of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainer via
GitHub direct contact (issue, discussion, or email if listed in the repo
profile). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and
fairly.

All maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.
